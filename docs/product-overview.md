# AssetFlow Product Overview

## What is AssetFlow?

AssetFlow is an asset management platform designed to help organizations track, manage, and maintain their physical assets in one centralized system.

The goal is to replace manual spreadsheets and disconnected processes with a simple and reliable system for managing asset information, assignments, and maintenance workflows.

## Who is it for?

AssetFlow is built for organizations that manage multiple assets and need better visibility into:

- What assets they own
- Who is using each asset
- Where assets are located
- When maintenance is required

The first target users are organizations, teams, and administrators responsible for managing company assets.

## MVP Features (Version 1)

The first version focuses on the core asset management workflow:

### Asset Management
- Create and manage assets
- View asset details
- Update asset information
- Track asset status

### Employee Management
- Manage employees/users
- Assign assets to employees
- View assignment history

### Asset Assignments
- Track who has which asset
- Monitor assigned and available assets

### Maintenance Tracking
- Record maintenance activities
- Track asset maintenance status

### Dashboard
- Provide an overview of assets and important information
- Show key statistics such as total, available, assigned, and maintenance assets
- Display recent activities

## Future Improvements

Future versions may include:

- Role-based permissions
- Organization management
- Advanced reporting
- Billing/subscription features
- More automation and integrations

## Technical Direction

The project is being structured as a scalable SaaS-ready application using:

- Next.js
- TypeScript
- Turborepo monorepo architecture
- Shared UI packages
- Internationalization support
-  Design system with reusable UI patterns

## Current Development Focus 

The current development focus is building the foundation of AssetFlow with a scalable architecture, including internationalization, design system, reusable components, and a SaaS-ready structure before implementing full product features.
